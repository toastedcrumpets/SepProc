  • Students performing the distillation lab will have to wait for the analysis of their samples via the GC.

I expect to see citations, an abstract, conclusions, and plenty of reasoning. Your report should cover no more than 10 sides of A4 (this includes everything, e.g. titles, blank pages, appendices - no exceptions). Key to achieving high marks in the report is remaining concise, consistent, and covering all relevant topics and reasoning. Do not repeat any material from the handouts (I do not want to write my procedure again, I am very familiar with it), you may put a brief description of the experimental method but only go into details about anything you think was key (i.e., something which may explain your results). The report should tell me how you managed to calculate as much interesting information about the performance of the column as you can. I.e., I want to know about how many theoretical stages each column is capable of performing, heat losses, efficiencies, mass flows, etc. and how/why your results are correct. If you cannot tell me an exact value for something then tell me the range in which it must lie. The narrower you can successfully determine the range the better. Remember, I'm not looking for a particular value or measurement, or answer. I want evidence that you understand what the column is doing and that you understand the contents of the course.
Academic writing should always be written as impersonal present tense. This forces you to explain the results more clearly. For example,
Bad: We went to the column and I read through the instructions provided to operate the column
Good: The experiments are carried out according to the lab procedure (see Ref. [1]).
Bad: The efficiency was lower than expected as we made a mistake in setting the water flowrate.
Good: The water flow-rate is set to 0.9 l/hr during operation which is lower than the 1.2 l/hr value recommended in the lab procedure and may explain the reduced efficiency. This changes the slope of the operating line...
